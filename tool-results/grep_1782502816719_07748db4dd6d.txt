voilà!</figcaption></figure><h3 id=\"d74d\" class=\"nz oa hp bb ob oc od ec oe of og ee oh nm oi oj ok nq ol om on nu oo op oq or bg\" data-selectable-paragraph=\"\">Let’s summarize this</h3><p id=\"c9ee\" class=\"pw-post-body-paragraph nb nc hp nd b ne os ng nh ni ot nk nl nm ou no np nq ov ns nt nu ow nw nx ny hi bg\" data-selectable-paragraph=\"\">Getting around as an illegal streaming host is becoming so complex that it has got simpler to try to hide the content on third party servers of the internet giants (I found the same behaviour on <a class=\"z qa\" href=\"https://vk.com/\" rel=\"noopener ugc nofollow\" target=\"_blank\">VK</a> servers without the PNG trick) which offer great infrastructure at a reasonable price.</p><p id=\"b78a\" class=\"pw-post-body-paragraph nb nc hp nd b ne nf ng nh ni nj nk nl nm nn no np nq nr ns nt nu nv nw nx ny hi bg\" data-selectable-paragraph=\"\">The recipe is pretty straightforward:</p><p id=\"e523\" class=\"pw-post-body-paragraph nb nc hp nd b ne nf ng nh ni nj nk nl nm nn no np nq nr ns nt nu nv nw nx ny hi bg\" data-selectable-paragraph=\"\">1. Create an HLS from your MP4 source video. This means, take the original video, cut it in ~300 chunks of 5MB and create an index for these files.<br>I found an example of this command <a class=\"z qa\" href=\"https://www.keycdn.com/support/how-to-convert-mp4-to-hls\" rel=\"noopener ugc nofollow\" target=\"_blank\">here</a>:</p><pre class=\"pa pb pc pd pe pm pn po pp ae pq bg\"><span id=\"b694\" class=\"nz oa hp pn b pr ps pt e pu pv\" data-selectable-paragraph=\"\">ffmpeg -i input.mp4 -profile:v baseline -level 3.0 -s 640x360 -start_number 0 -hls_time 10 -hls_list_size 0 -f hls index.m3u8</span></pre><p id=\"0c03\" class=\"pw-post-body-paragraph nb nc hp nd b ne nf ng nh ni nj nk nl nm nn no np nq nr ns nt nu nv nw nx ny hi bg\" data-selectable-paragraph=\"\">2. For each chunk, add this 1x1 pixel before the actual data in order to hide the real content. Congrats, the video chunk has been transformed into an image! 🎉</p><p id=\"2631\" class=\"pw-post-body-paragraph nb nc hp nd b ne nf ng nh ni nj nk nl nm nn no np nq nr ns nt nu nv nw nx ny hi bg\" data-selectable-paragraph=\"\">3. Upload every “image” to Google servers. I guess you could send it via the Google <a class=\"z qa\" href=\"https://developers.google.com/drive\" rel=\"noopener ugc nofollow\" target=\"_blank\">Drive API</a> but I’m not sure of that.</p><p id=\"fde6\" class=\"pw-post-body-paragraph nb nc hp nd b ne nf ng nh ni nj nk nl nm nn no np nq nr ns nt nu nv nw nx ny hi bg\" data-selectable-paragraph=\"\">4. Update your m3u8 index file with the links to each chunk the Google API saved them at. This is why I highlighted that working with unpredictable chunk file locations was more interesting than it sounded.</p><p id=\"c320\" class=\"pw-post-body-paragraph nb nc hp nd b ne nf ng nh ni nj nk nl nm nn no np nq nr ns nt nu nv nw nx ny hi bg\" data-selectable-paragraph=\"\">5. Host the m3u8 text file anywhere and link the video player to it.</p><h3 id=\"7f08\" class=\"nz oa hp bb ob oc od ec oe of og ee oh nm oi oj ok nq ol om on nu oo op oq or bg\" data-selectable-paragraph=\"\">Conclusion</h3><p id=\"bc69\" class=\"pw-post-body-paragraph nb nc hp nd b ne os ng nh ni ot nk nl nm ou no np nq ov ns nt nu ow nw nx ny hi bg\" data-selectable-paragraph=\"\">I find it fascinating how this cat and mouse game is evolving. *Grabs Popcorn*</p><p id=\"9a57\" class=\"pw-post-body-paragraph nb nc hp nd b ne nf ng nh ni nj nk nl nm nn no np nq nr ns nt nu nv nw nx ny hi bg\" data-selectable-paragraph=\"\"><strong class=\"nd hq\">BUT</strong>,<strong class=\"nd hq\"> </strong>I have to express my disappointment.<br>Yes that’s a smart move to embed the video payload behind a PNG but you can do so much more as a mouse! 🐭</p><p id=\"56c2\" class=\"pw-post-body-paragraph nb nc hp nd b ne nf ng nh ni nj nk nl nm nn no np nq nr ns nt nu nv nw nx ny hi bg\" data-selectable-paragraph=\"\">I did some tests with encryption and embedded the payload as the image content itself, resulting in cooler scrambled pictures. I will try to write an article about all that if you find this interesting.</p><p id=\"5695\" class=\"pw-post-body-paragraph nb nc hp nd b ne nf ng nh ni nj nk nl nm nn no np nq nr ns nt nu nv nw nx ny hi bg\" data-selectable-paragraph=\"\">On the other hand, I also wrote some algorithms to detect these suspicious behaviours and I am preparing an article on that as well. That will be the 🐱 part !</p><p id=\"70fe\" class=\"pw-post-body-paragraph nb nc hp nd b ne nf ng nh ni nj nk nl nm nn no np nq nr ns nt nu nv nw nx ny hi bg\" data-selectable-paragraph=\"\"><em
